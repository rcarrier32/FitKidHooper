Fit Kid Hooper — Privacy Notice

Plain-language summary of what we collect and why. Last updated 2026-07-17.

⚠ Draft pending legal review. This notice is being finalized with a COPPA-savvy attorney before public launch.

Who this is for

Fit Kid Hooper is a basketball training app for young athletes (roughly ages 9–14). Because our athletes are children, a parent or guardian must give permission before an account is created. We ask for the parent's email and consent at sign-up.

What we collect

  • From the athlete: a Jersey Name (username) they choose, a 6-digit passcode, and the training info they enter (favorite players, position, goals, birthday for the right age group) plus their in-app activity (practices, streaks, badges, XP).
  • From the parent/guardian: an email address, used to verify consent and to keep the parent informed about their athlete's progress.
  • Automatically: anonymous usage events (what screens are used, when) to improve the app. These are tied to the account but never include a child's contact information.

What we do with it

  • Save the athlete's progress so they never lose their streak, badges, or XP.
  • Let them appear on Challenges leaderboards using their first name and last initial only.
  • Email the parent about their athlete (for example, if they haven't trained in a while).
  • Understand which features help kids train, so we can make the app better.

What we do NOT do

  • We do not sell personal information.
  • We do not show third-party advertising to children.
  • We do not collect a child's email address or precise location.

Parents are in control

A parent or guardian may review their athlete's information, ask us to delete the account and its data, or withdraw consent at any time. To do so, contact us at the email below.
