# Fit Kid Hooper Product Principles

These principles define Fit Kid Hooper's long-term product philosophy and decision-making. They should change very rarely.

If a proposed feature conflicts with these principles, stop and ask the Founder for a product decision before implementation.

## Mission

Fit Kid Hooper exists to help young athletes become better basketball players through consistent, enjoyable daily practice.

Every product decision should reinforce that mission.

## Core Philosophy

We do not build features. We build habits.

The goal is not to maximize screen time. The goal is to maximize time practicing basketball.

The app should motivate kids to put the phone down and train.

## Athlete First

The athlete is always the primary user.

Parents, coaches, and organizations are future layers built around the athlete experience.

When priorities conflict, optimize for the athlete first.

## Athlete Identity

The app should always answer:

- Who am I?
- Who am I becoming?
- What should I work on next?
- Why should I practice today?

Every major screen should reinforce one or more of these questions.

## Overview vs. Profile

Overview is the athlete's dashboard. It tells the athlete where they are today.

Profile represents the athlete's identity.

Settings configure the application.

Never confuse these concepts.

## Settings

Settings are for configuring the app. They should contain items such as:

- Notifications
- Theme
- Privacy
- Account
- Backup and restore
- About
- Feedback
- Support Legends

Athlete identity does not belong in Settings.

## My Player

Kids think in terms of "My Player."

Identity includes:

- Avatar
- Name
- Jersey number
- Favorite players
- Position
- Goals
- Experience
- Dominant hand

These belong to the athlete experience.

## Motivation Before Statistics

Progress should feel meaningful. Do not overwhelm athletes with numbers.

Whenever possible, show:

- Identity
- Improvement
- Milestones
- Next goal

Prefer these over raw statistics.

## Simplicity

If multiple systems accomplish the same goal, prefer extending an existing system over creating a new one.

- Avoid duplicate progression systems.
- Avoid duplicate navigation.
- Avoid duplicate settings.

## Daily Habit Loop

The ideal experience is:

> Open app → Know what to do → Practice → Feel successful → Return tomorrow

Every feature should strengthen this loop.

## Product Decisions

When making product decisions, ask:

- Does this make practicing more likely?
- Does this reduce friction?
- Does this simplify the experience?
- Does this help the athlete feel like they are improving?

If not, reconsider the feature.

## User Validation

Real athlete feedback overrides assumptions.

Playwright findings, analytics, and user interviews should influence future product decisions.

Build from evidence rather than opinion whenever possible.

## Product Consistency

Do not redesign experiences unnecessarily.

Prefer refining and polishing existing experiences. Protect consistency across the application.

## Definition of Success

Success is not measured by:

- Features shipped
- Lines of code
- Screens added

Success is measured by:

- Athletes returning
- Athletes practicing
- Athletes improving
- Athletes enjoying the experience

These principles guide all future product discussions, UX decisions, and implementation work.
